Abstract

Indoor autonomous vehicles and drones require a comprehensive understanding of indoor environments for tasks such as localization, navigation, and executing other complex missions. Typically, RGB information is captured using cameras or similar sensors, and deep learning-based semantic segmentation techniques are employed for pixel-level scene understanding. However, indoor environments also contain rich information beyond the visible light spectrum, which has been largely overlooked in existing research. To fully leverage multispectral data, this study introduces IndoorMS, a multispectral dataset for indoor scene understanding. Using a multispectral sensor, we collected images from diverse indoor settings—including meeting rooms, halls, lounges, offices, corridors, and classrooms—across 17 buildings, providing semantic segmentation annotations with 19 fine-grained categories. Benchmark experiments were conducted using multiple representative semantic segmentation frameworks, and the results were discussed. This work is the first to support research on indoor multispectral scene understanding with a dedicated dataset. Key challenges and future directions are also highlighted.
